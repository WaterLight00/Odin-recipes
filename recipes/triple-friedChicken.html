Triple Dipped Chicken

Two pieces of crispy flaky fried chicken

This fried chicken batter yields the crispiest, spiciest, homemade fried chicken I have ever tasted. it has been a picnic favorite in my family for years and taste equally good served hot or cold.

Ingredients

  • 1 quart of vegetable oil
  • 4 1/3 cups all-purpose flour, devided
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons garlic salt
  • 1 tablespoon of ground balck pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon of poultry seasoning
  • 1 1/2 cups of beer as needed
  • 2 egg yolks, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground balck pepper
  • 1(3 pound) whole chicken, cut into pieces

Cooking Steps

  1. Heat oil in a deep fryer to 350 degrees (175 degrees C)
  2. Mix 3 cups of flour, 1 tablespoon of pepper, paprika, and poultry seasoning together in a medium bowl.
  3. Stir 1 1/2 cups of beer, remaining 1 1/3 cups of flour, egg yolks, salt, and 1/4 teaspoon pepper togetherin a separate bowl; thin with more beer if the batter is too thick.
  4. Moisten chicken pieces in a little water, the dip in seasoned flour mixture. shake off excess and dip in beer batter, then dip in seasoned mixture once more.
  5. Lower chicken pieces carefully into the oil in batches. fry until crispy and well browned, about 15 minutes to 18 minutes. An instant-read termometer inserted near the bone should read 165 degrees F(74 degrees C). Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate to drain.
