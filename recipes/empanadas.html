Crispy Golden Empanadas

This beef empanada recipe is made with Goya Discos pastry rounds and a mouthwatering filling of seasoned ground beef, tomatoes, onions, and garlic. Enjoy these flaky, deep-fried empanadas as an appetizer, a main dish, or even after your main course.

Ingredients

  • 1 Tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1/2 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup tomato sauce
  • 6 olives, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons sofrito
  • 1 pack of sazon with anato and coriander
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1/2 dried oregano
  • Ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 pack of Goya empanada disks
  • 1 quart of corn oil

Cooking Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large skilled over medium herat. Add ground beef; cook and stir until browned and crumbly, about 10 minutes. Add onios and cook until soft, about 5 minutes. Stir in tomato sauce, olives, sofrito, sazon, garlic, oregano, and pepper. Reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer until mixture thickens, about 15 minutes.
  2. Roll empanada disks on a lightly floured surface until 1/2 inch larger in diameter. Spoon about 1 tablespoon meat mixture into the middle of each disk. Moisten the Disk edges with water, fold in half over filling to form a half-moon, and pinch to seal( or seal with a fork).
  3. Heat 2 1/2 inches corn oil in a deep-fryer or large saucepan to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Working in batches, fry empanadas until crispy and golden brown, fliping once, 4 to 6 minutes. Transfer to paper towel to drain.
